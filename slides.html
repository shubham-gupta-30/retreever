ICML 2026 short talk

ReTreever paper presentation

Six-slide project story: why hierarchy helps, why existing hierarchical retrievers do not scale, how ReTreever learns a retrieval-optimized tree, and what the results show.

1

Title

Hierarchical Retrieval at Scale: Bridging Interpretability and Efficiency. ReTreever is designed to be as fast as dense retrieval while preserving the inspectability of hierarchical methods.

2

Why hierarchy?

Hierarchical retrieval organizes data into progressively finer semantic groups. This gives data organization and interpretability: users can probe nodes and paths to understand retrieval structure.

Data organizationInspectable nodesSemantic paths
3

The scaling problem

Methods such as RAPTOR rely on LLM calls to summarize and re-embed tree nodes. That makes them slow and expensive. ReTreever removes LLM calls by building and navigating the hierarchy entirely on embeddings.

4

The core idea

A frozen bi-encoder maps queries and documents to embeddings. A differentiable binary tree routes them through learned split nodes. Leaf-assignment probabilities become fine representations; intermediate levels become coarser representations.

5

Training

Each split node outputs a left/right routing probability. Probabilities multiply along paths, keeping routing differentiable. A contrastive loss pulls positive query-reference pairs toward the same leaf assignment and pushes negatives apart.

6

Results and scale

ReTreever is 160x faster than RAPTOR, improves average NDCG@10 by 20.7 points against RAPTOR, uses 0 LLM calls, and scales to the 124.5M-document Reddit Title-Body corpus where ScaNN exceeded 1 TB RAM.
