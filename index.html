ReTreever: Hierarchical Retrieval at Scale

Fast like dense retrieval. Inspectable like a hierarchy. ReTreever learns a retrieval-optimized tree over embeddings for scalable coarse-to-fine search.

Shubham Gupta, Zichao Li, Tianyi Chen, Cem Subakan, Siva Reddy, Perouz Taslakian, Valentina Zantedeschi

ServiceNow Research · Université Laval · Mila · McGill University

Motivation

Dense retrieval is fast, but opaque. Hierarchies are interpretable, but expensive.

ReTreever keeps the useful organization of tree-based retrieval while removing the LLM-bound bottleneck. It builds and navigates the hierarchy entirely over embeddings, so routing decisions are inspectable and search remains efficient.

Method

A tree learned end-to-end for retrieval

Each split node emits a soft left/right routing probability. Probabilities multiply along paths to produce leaf assignments. A bidirectional contrastive objective pulls positive query-reference pairs toward the same leaf while separating negatives.

1

Encode

Embed queries and references with a frozen encoder.

2

Route

Send embeddings through a differentiable binary tree.

3

Match

Compare leaf-assignment vectors for retrieval.

4

Inspect

Use paths and intermediate nodes as explanations.
