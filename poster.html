ICML 2026 poster overview

Hierarchical retrieval that scales

The poster story: hierarchical retrieval gives organization and interpretability, but LLM-bound approaches do not scale. ReTreever learns the hierarchy directly over embeddings, so it keeps inspectability while achieving low-latency retrieval.

Method

Encoder + differentiable tree organizer

Query and document embeddings are routed through a learned binary tree. Routing probabilities define coarse-to-fine representations and interpretable tree paths.

Key result

160x

faster than RAPTOR, while improving average NDCG@10 against RAPTOR by 20.7 points.

Search modes

Single-index or multi-index

Single-index search scans a chosen representation level; multi-index search routes the query to top leaves and only searches relevant leaf indexes.

Scale

124.5M documents

On Reddit Title-Body, ReTreever provides a memory-friendly alternative to global ANN; ScaNN exceeded 1 TB RAM in the scale setting.

Architecture: a frozen bi-encoder plus differentiable tree organizer, trained by a contrastive objective over query/reference assignments.
